Guest lecturer

Dr. Vivan Joseph

Vivan has a PhD in Philosophy and an MA in Philosophy of Mind from Warwick University and did his undergraduate studies in philosophy at UCL.

His doctoral thesis looked at the relationship between attention and awareness in visual experience. The main idea is that though claims emerging from psychological research on selective attention appear to challenge our pre-theoretical understanding of both attention and awareness, it is possible to retain our pre-theoretical understanding in the face of these challenges.

Before starting his PhD, Vivan worked for a mental health charity for some years, which has left him with an enduring interest in a diverse range of mental health-related topics. Between 2012 and 2015 he convened and taught on an interdisciplinary module Navigating Psychopathology, which incorporated a variety of perspectives on mental health
